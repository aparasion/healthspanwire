**Twentyeight Health** has launched **Complete Care**, a pioneering insurance-enabled membership model aimed at providing **unlimited, real-time access** to comprehensive women’s healthcare services. This innovative platform partners with over **100 insurance providers**, including Medicaid, to cover approximately **16 million women** across the U.S. The model allows members to access a range of services, from hormonal birth control and STI treatment to urgent care and skincare, often at little to no cost through in-network insurance plans.

The significance of Complete Care lies in its **holistic approach** to women’s health, addressing the ongoing nature of care rather than treating it as a one-time solution. CEO Bruno Van Tuykom emphasizes that women often require adjustments and follow-ups across various treatments, which Complete Care facilitates through a unified platform. Members can communicate with providers without appointments, manage prescriptions, and receive medications directly at home or their local pharmacy, enhancing the overall care experience. The membership is available for **$19.99 per month** for those paying out of pocket, while in-network users typically incur only their standard copay, which can be as low as **$0**.

The launch of Complete Care represents a significant shift in the landscape of women’s healthcare, particularly in the telehealth space. By expanding to over **500 insurance plans** by year-end, Twentyeight Health is poised to reach more than **50 million women**, reinforcing the need for **personalized and accessible healthcare solutions**. This model not only streamlines the patient experience but also sets a new standard for how digital health platforms can integrate insurance coverage with comprehensive care, potentially influencing future drug development and healthcare delivery models in the longevity and healthspan research fields.
