Tristan Schukraft, Founder and CEO of MISTR, has joined the producing team of the Broadway musical **Titanique**, a comedic reinterpretation of the classic film *Titanic* featuring the music of Céline Dion. Known for his extensive contributions to LGBTQ+ culture and healthcare through MISTR, the largest telehealth platform for HIV prevention and long-term care, Schukraft aims to leverage this theatrical collaboration to enhance PrEP awareness and drive ticket sales. The partnership will include integrated marketing campaigns and co-branded content that connect the show with diverse audiences.

The significance of this collaboration lies in its potential to reshape public perceptions around HIV prevention, particularly within the LGBTQ+ community. MISTR's involvement in **Titanique** allows for innovative outreach strategies that utilize the musical's broad appeal to promote healthcare initiatives. By activating events at Tryst Hospitality venues and creating engaging content, Schukraft is positioning MISTR as a critical player in both entertainment and public health, fostering a sense of community while addressing serious health issues.

This initiative exemplifies a shift in how healthcare organizations can engage with cultural phenomena to enhance their impact. By intertwining theatrical experiences with health education, MISTR could set a precedent for future collaborations between healthcare and the arts, potentially accelerating timelines for awareness campaigns and broadening the reach of critical health messaging. As the landscape of health communication evolves, this model may pave the way for similar partnerships, reinforcing the importance of community engagement in promoting healthspan and longevity.
