Researchers found that aerobic exercises like walking, cycling, and swimming are the most effective ways to relieve knee arthritis pain. A comprehensive review of 217 clinical trials revealed that these activities significantly reduce pain and improve movement for those suffering from knee osteoarthritis. While other types of exercise, such as strength training and mind-body practices, can be beneficial, they work best when combined with aerobic workouts.

This discovery is crucial for anyone looking to manage knee pain and maintain an active lifestyle as they age. Nearly 30% of adults over 45 show signs of knee osteoarthritis, and many experience severe symptoms. Engaging in regular aerobic exercise can lead to better physical function, improved walking ability, and an overall enhanced quality of life. For people with knee osteoarthritis, incorporating these activities into their routine can make a significant difference in managing their condition.

The evidence comes from a large-scale analysis involving 15,684 participants, making it a strong basis for the recommendations. The review assessed various forms of exercise over different time frames, confirming that aerobic exercise consistently outperformed other types in terms of pain relief and functional improvement. Importantly, no exercise type was linked to an increased risk of adverse events, supporting the notion that exercise is a safe and essential part of treatment for knee osteoarthritis.

If you're looking to ease knee pain, consider integrating walking, cycling, or swimming into your weekly routine. These activities not only help manage pain but also promote better mobility and overall well-being. Always consult with a healthcare provider to tailor an exercise plan that suits your individual needs and limitations.
