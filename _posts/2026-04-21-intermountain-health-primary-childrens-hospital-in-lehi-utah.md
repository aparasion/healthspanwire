Intermountain Health Primary Children’s Hospital - Miller Family Campus in Lehi, Utah, has rapidly established itself as a vital hub for pediatric trauma and specialty care since its opening in 2024. In just two years, the facility has managed over **30,000 patient days** and **39,872 emergency department visits**, demonstrating its critical role in serving the healthcare needs of children in the expanding communities of Utah County and Southern Salt Lake County. The recent addition of **eight new medical-surgical beds** and plans for six newborn ICU rooms will boost inpatient capacity by approximately **20%**, further enhancing access to timely and specialized care.

The hospital's impact is underscored by real-world outcomes, such as the case of 17-year-old Max Braithwaite, whose life was saved when he was rerouted to the Miller Family Campus during a medical emergency. This incident highlights the importance of local access to specialized pediatric services, which can significantly reduce critical response times. Additionally, the hospital has introduced specialty programs like the **Pectus Center of Excellence** and a new sports medicine initiative, indicating a commitment to comprehensive care that addresses both acute and chronic conditions.

The expansion of services and facilities at Intermountain Primary Children’s Hospital - Miller Family Campus represents a shift in pediatric healthcare delivery, emphasizing the need for localized care that can adapt to community growth. This model not only alleviates the burden on distant facilities but also fosters a more integrated approach to pediatric health, potentially influencing future healthcare infrastructure planning and service delivery strategies in similar regions.
