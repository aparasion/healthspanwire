The **2026 Main Residency Match** has achieved a historic milestone, with **44,344 training positions** offered across over **6,800 program tracks**, marking a **2.6% increase** from the previous year. This year saw more than **38,000 physicians** successfully match to PGY-1 positions, including a notable rise in programs that provide **hospital-sponsored disability insurance** without medical underwriting. In response, Set for Life Insurance has launched a resource specifically aimed at guiding medical residents through the complexities of **Guaranteed Standard Issue (GSI) disability insurance**, which is crucial for securing coverage during their training.

The significance of this development lies in the **financial protection** it affords residents. GSI policies require no medical examination or health history questions, allowing residents to access essential income protection without the risk of higher premiums typically associated with fully underwritten policies. Residents who enroll during their training can also increase their monthly benefits without future medical underwriting, a critical feature given the financial pressures they face. The resource provides tailored information on eligibility, benefit limits, and enrollment timelines for **18 major medical institutions**, ensuring that residents can make informed decisions before the enrollment window closes.

The takeaway from this initiative is its potential to reshape how residents approach disability insurance during their training. By addressing common pitfalls—such as applying for fully underwritten policies before exhausting GSI options—this resource aims to prevent costly mistakes that could jeopardize their financial security post-training. As the landscape of residency programs evolves, integrating GSI coverage into financial planning will be essential for safeguarding the long-term health and career stability of future physicians.
