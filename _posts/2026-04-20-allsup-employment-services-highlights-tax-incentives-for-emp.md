Allsup Employment Services (AES) is advocating for the utilization of **federal tax incentives** as a strategic approach for employers to enhance workforce inclusivity for individuals with disabilities. These incentives, part of a broader initiative to promote accessibility in the workplace, can significantly offset the costs associated with hiring and accommodating qualified candidates experiencing disabilities. The **Ticket to Work Program (TTW)** plays a crucial role in this ecosystem, providing eligible individuals receiving Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI) with resources to explore employment opportunities while maintaining their benefits.

The significance of these findings lies in their potential to reshape employer attitudes toward hiring individuals with disabilities. By leveraging tax provisions such as the **Disabled Access Credit** and the **Work Opportunity Tax Credit (WOTC)**, employers can not only reduce financial barriers but also tap into a diverse talent pool that enhances workforce resilience. For instance, the WOTC offers credits ranging from **$1,200 to $9,600** based on the employee's background and duration of employment, incentivizing businesses to adopt inclusive hiring practices. This approach aligns with broader goals of improving workplace diversity and retention, ultimately benefiting organizational culture and performance.

The practical implication for the longevity science and healthspan research fields is clear: integrating individuals with disabilities into the workforce can foster a more diverse range of perspectives and skills, which is essential for innovation in research and development. As organizations in this sector increasingly prioritize inclusivity, understanding and utilizing available tax incentives could expedite the timeline for building diverse research teams, thereby enhancing the overall impact of longevity science initiatives.
