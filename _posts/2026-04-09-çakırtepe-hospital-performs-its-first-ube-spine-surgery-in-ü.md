Çakırtepe Hospital in Ünye, Türkiye, has successfully performed its inaugural **Unilateral Biportal Endoscopy (UBE)** procedure, marking a significant advancement in minimally invasive spine surgery within the region. This technique, executed by Orthopedics and Spine Surgery Specialist Op. Dr. Mehmet Onur Sarıhan, is designed to minimize tissue trauma, enhance patient comfort, and facilitate quicker recovery times compared to traditional surgical methods.

The introduction of UBE at Çakırtepe Hospital not only expands access to advanced spinal care for local patients but also reduces the need for travel to larger metropolitan centers for specialized treatment. This development underscores the hospital's commitment to high-quality medical care and its role as a regional hub for advanced surgical techniques. By integrating UBE into its offerings, the hospital enhances its capability to deliver effective, patient-centered care, which is essential for improving health outcomes in spine surgery.

The implications of this milestone extend beyond immediate patient benefits; it signals a shift in the landscape of healthcare delivery in Türkiye. As Çakırtepe Hospital continues to develop its health tourism initiatives, the availability of sophisticated procedures like UBE may attract international patients seeking quality care at lower costs than in Western markets. This trend could accelerate the adoption of minimally invasive techniques across the region, influencing both research paradigms and clinical practices in spine surgery and other specialties.
