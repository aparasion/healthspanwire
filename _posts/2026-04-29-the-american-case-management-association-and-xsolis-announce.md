Researchers honored two remarkable individuals with the 2026 Case Management Hero Award for their exceptional dedication to patient care. Joan Brueggeman, a seasoned case manager, and Alberto Rangel, who was recognized posthumously, exemplified compassion and advocacy in their work. Rangel tragically lost his life while serving at a hospital, highlighting the risks that healthcare workers often face. His legacy will continue through the Alberto Rangel Legacy Fund, which supports vital services for Spanish-speaking patients and older adults.

This recognition is significant for anyone interested in the quality of care they receive as they age. The work of case managers like Brueggeman and Rangel directly impacts patient outcomes. Their commitment to compassionate care can lead to better support for individuals navigating complex health challenges, including older adults managing chronic conditions. Their efforts help ensure that patients receive the attention and resources they need, which is essential for maintaining health and well-being.

The award ceremony reflects a growing recognition of the challenges faced by healthcare professionals. While the event honors individuals, it also serves as a reminder of the broader issues within the healthcare system. The recognition of Rangel and Brueggeman comes from the American Case Management Association (ACMA), which has been supporting case management professionals since 1999. This year’s awards highlight the importance of resilience and teamwork in healthcare, especially in light of personal adversities faced by these heroes.

As you consider your own health journey, remember the importance of compassionate care. Seek out healthcare providers who prioritize collaboration and patient-centered approaches. This can lead to better health outcomes and a more supportive experience as you navigate your own health challenges.
