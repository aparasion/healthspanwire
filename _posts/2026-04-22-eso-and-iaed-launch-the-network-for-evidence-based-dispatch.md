**ESO Solutions, Inc.** and **The International Academies of Emergency Dispatch (IAED)** have launched the **Network for Evidence-Based Dispatch (NEBD)**, a groundbreaking research consortium aimed at enhancing emergency dispatch decision-making through continuous, outcomes-based evidence. Co-led by Dr. Brent Myers of ESO and Jerry Overton of IAED, the NEBD comprises over a dozen EMS and fire agencies that will collaborate to refine protocols and improve patient outcomes based on real-world data. This initiative builds on a strategic alliance established in November 2025, signaling a commitment to advancing evidence-based practices in emergency response.

The significance of the NEBD lies in its potential to transform how emergency dispatch decisions are made. Traditionally, dispatchers operate without feedback on patient outcomes post-call, which limits the ability to optimize protocols. By leveraging a standing group of agencies, the NEBD will facilitate a continuous research loop, allowing for rapid iteration on critical questions such as alternative dispatch protocols and common chief complaints. This approach not only aims to enhance operational efficiency but also to establish best practices that are informed by empirical data, thereby driving stronger outcomes in emergency medical services.

The implication for the field is substantial: the NEBD represents a shift towards a more integrated, data-driven framework for emergency response. By fostering collaboration among diverse agencies, the consortium will enable the rapid dissemination of findings that can inform policy and practice at scale. This initiative could significantly shorten drug development timelines and improve the efficacy of interventions in emergency settings, ultimately leading to better health outcomes for patients and more effective resource allocation within the EMS community.
