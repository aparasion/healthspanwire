**Joyce University of Nursing and Health Sciences** has achieved the prestigious **2026 USA Today Top Workplaces award** for the third consecutive year, underscoring its commitment to a **people-first culture** in nursing education. This recognition is based on authentic employee feedback collected through a confidential survey by Energage, highlighting the university's focus on fostering an environment where staff feel **valued and supported**. Ann Johnson, Vice President of People and Culture, emphasized that the university's mission is intertwined with the well-being of its employees, ultimately benefiting the students and the communities they serve.

The significance of this accolade lies in its reflection of **employee engagement** and workplace satisfaction, which are critical indicators of organizational performance. With over **42,000 organizations** invited to participate, the award showcases Joyce University's dedication to creating a supportive atmosphere that prioritizes employee feedback. Eric Rubino, CEO of Energage, noted that such recognition is a testament to the credibility of the organization and its leadership, which is increasingly sought after by job seekers and customers alike. This commitment to employee well-being is particularly relevant in the healthcare sector, where staff morale can directly impact patient care quality.

The takeaway from this achievement is its potential to influence **nursing education** and workforce development strategies. As Joyce University expands its reach across multiple states, maintaining a robust internal culture will be essential for attracting and retaining talent in a competitive market. This model of prioritizing employee satisfaction may serve as a blueprint for other institutions aiming to enhance their educational impact while addressing the growing demand for qualified healthcare professionals.
