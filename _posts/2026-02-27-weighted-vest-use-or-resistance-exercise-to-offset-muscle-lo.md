The INVEST in Bone Health Trial has revealed critical insights into the interplay between weight loss and musculoskeletal health, particularly emphasizing the benefits of resistance training. In this study, 150 participants aged around 66 years were randomized into three groups: weight loss (WL), WL plus resistance training (WL + RT), and WL plus weighted vest use (WL + VEST). Over 12 months, all groups achieved approximately 10% weight loss, but only the WL + RT group demonstrated significant increases in mid-thigh muscle cross-sectional area and density, alongside reductions in intermuscular adipose tissue and fat mass.

These findings are pivotal for the longevity and healthspan field, as they highlight the importance of resistance training in mitigating muscle loss associated with weight reduction. While weight loss generally correlates with improved muscle density, it can also lead to decreased muscle quantity, underscoring the need for targeted interventions in older adults with obesity.

A key takeaway from this research is the effectiveness of resistance training as a strategy for preserving muscle quality during weight loss, suggesting that integrating such programs into weight management protocols could enhance overall musculoskeletal health in aging populations.
