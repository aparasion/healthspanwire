Philanthropists Ernest and Evelyn Rady have donated $75 million to Scripps Health, significantly enhancing the Scripps Memorial Hospital La Jolla by renaming its North Tower the Rady Tower. This eight-story facility, which opened just 10 months ago, is designed to provide advanced medical care with features like private patient rooms and cutting-edge operating technology. The Radys, who have a long history of philanthropy in San Diego, aim to support high-quality healthcare in their community, continuing a legacy started by Ellen Browning Scripps over a century ago.

This generous donation is particularly important for individuals seeking better healthcare options as they age. The Rady Tower offers comprehensive mother-baby services and a healing environment, which can improve patient experiences and outcomes. With Scripps Health treating over 650,000 patients annually, this investment not only enhances the hospital's capacity but also ensures that residents have access to world-class medical services, which is crucial for maintaining health and well-being as one gets older.

The Rady's contribution is part of a larger 25-year master plan to transform the Scripps campus into a leading medical center. While the Rady Tower is now operational, the full impact of this investment will unfold over time as the facility continues to serve the community. This commitment to improving healthcare infrastructure demonstrates how philanthropy can play a vital role in advancing public health, particularly in a rapidly growing population.

As you look for ways to support your health and longevity, consider the importance of community healthcare resources. Engaging with local health initiatives or supporting local hospitals can contribute to better health outcomes for you and your neighbors.
