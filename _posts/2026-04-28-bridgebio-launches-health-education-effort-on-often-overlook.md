Researchers have launched a new campaign to raise awareness about ATTR-CM, a serious heart condition that is often misdiagnosed. This initiative, featuring actor Morgan Freeman and heart health advocate Howard “H” White, aims to educate the public on recognizing symptoms like shortness of breath, fatigue, and leg swelling, which can be mistaken for normal aging or other heart issues. The campaign emphasizes that early diagnosis and treatment can significantly improve health outcomes for individuals over 55, particularly in communities that have been disproportionately affected.

For those looking to age well, understanding ATTR-CM is crucial. This condition is linked to the buildup of a protein called transthyretin (TTR) in the heart, which can lead to a thicker, stiffer heart that struggles to pump blood effectively. Recent research indicates that individuals with ATTR-CM who start treatment early can experience a 50% reduction in hospitalizations due to heart-related issues and an overall better quality of life. This is particularly important for Black men and women, who may carry genetic variants that increase their risk and delay diagnosis.

The campaign is based on findings from a Phase 3 clinical trial of a treatment called Attruby, which showed promising results in both wild-type and hereditary forms of ATTR-CM. While the evidence is strong, it’s important to note that awareness and early recognition of the disease are still critical. Many people remain undiagnosed until their symptoms become severe. The initiative encourages individuals to advocate for their heart health and consult with healthcare providers if they experience concerning symptoms.

To support this effort, educational resources are available online, including a guide to help individuals discuss potential symptoms with their doctors. Early action can lead to better long-term outcomes, making it essential for people to stay informed and proactive about their heart health.
