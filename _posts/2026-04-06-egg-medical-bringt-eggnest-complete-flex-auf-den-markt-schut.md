Egg Medical has launched the **EggNest™ Complete Flex**, a groundbreaking radiation protection technology that enables medical professionals to operate safely without lead aprons or with ultra-lightweight lead shields. This innovative system requires no structural modifications, permitting seamless integration into existing medical environments. The first live operation utilizing Complete Flex will be performed by Dr. Jasvindar Singh on April 8, 2026, at Barnes-Jewish Hospital during the ARCH 2026 conference.

The significance of this development lies in its potential to enhance **occupational safety** for healthcare workers while maintaining operational efficiency. Traditional radiation protection systems often necessitate extensive renovations and can lead to costly downtime. In contrast, the EggNest Complete Flex allows for uninterrupted laboratory operations during installation, thereby safeguarding potential revenue streams. Additionally, the system reduces the physical strain on medical staff by minimizing the need for cumbersome protective gear, which can lead to long-term joint injuries.

The introduction of the EggNest Complete Flex could redefine current practices in radiation protection within medical settings. By addressing the dual challenges of staff safety and operational continuity, this technology may accelerate the adoption of **enhanced radiation protection devices (ERPDs)** across healthcare facilities. As hospitals face increasing pressure to improve employee retention and reduce workplace hazards, the rapid deployment of this system—currently in fifteen labs with plans for eighty more by the end of Q2—signals a significant shift in the approach to radiation safety in clinical environments.
