DeepL AI Labs has unveiled a groundbreaking approach to translating formatted documents, addressing a longstanding challenge in the localization industry. Their new "reconstruction" method allows for the accurate translation of complex document types—such as scanned PDFs and intricate layouts—by converting each page into an image and utilizing advanced Vision Language Models (VLM) to extract text while preserving the original formatting.

This innovation is significant for localization professionals as it reduces manual corrections and enhances data extraction accuracy from difficult documents, such as aged contracts or low-resolution scans. By leveraging VLM technology, DeepL is not only improving translation quality but also expanding its capabilities to handle multi-modal content, which includes text, images, and interactive elements.

For localization teams, this development means more reliable workflows and the ability to tackle a wider range of document types efficiently. I recommend checking out the full article for an in-depth look at how this technology can transform your document translation processes.
