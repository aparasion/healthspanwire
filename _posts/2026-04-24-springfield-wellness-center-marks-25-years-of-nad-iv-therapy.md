Springfield Wellness Center has marked a significant milestone by celebrating its 25th anniversary as a pioneering clinic in intravenous **NAD+ treatment** for addiction recovery. Founded in 2001 by Paula Norris Mestayer and Dr. Richard F. Mestayer III, the clinic has developed the proprietary **BR+NAD® protocol**, which combines NAD+ infusions with a comprehensive 10-day outpatient program. This program has attracted patients nationwide, showcasing the growing interest in NAD+ as a therapeutic agent in addiction recovery.

The **BR+NAD® protocol** is notable for its integrative approach, incorporating not only NAD+ infusions but also **counseling, hyperbaric oxygen therapy, photobiomodulation, PEMF**, and **auricular cool-laser therapy**. This multi-faceted treatment strategy aims to enhance recovery outcomes by addressing both the physiological and psychological aspects of addiction. The clinic's expansion into three distinct entities—patient care, physician training through BR+MD Consultants, and clinical research via NAD Research, Inc.—highlights its commitment to advancing the understanding and application of NAD+ in clinical settings. The annual **BR+NAD® Brain Restoration Summit** serves as a critical platform for sharing insights and fostering collaboration among professionals in the field.

The implications of Springfield Wellness Center's advancements are far-reaching. By expanding patient education and physician training, the clinic is poised to elevate standards in NAD+ therapy, ensuring that practitioners are well-informed about the nuances of medically supervised treatments versus unregulated alternatives. This initiative not only enhances the credibility of NAD+ therapies but also sets a benchmark for future research and clinical applications in the longevity and healthspan sectors, potentially accelerating the adoption of NAD+ as a mainstream therapeutic modality.
