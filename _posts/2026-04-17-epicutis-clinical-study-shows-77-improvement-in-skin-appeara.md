Epicutis has launched **Hydrobiome Serum**, a professional skincare treatment featuring a patented **TCP molecule**. A recent double-blind, placebo-controlled study conducted by Princeton Consumer Research involving 48 subjects over 8 weeks demonstrated that participants using the TCP formulation experienced a **77% improvement in overall skin appearance**, significantly outperforming the **56% improvement** observed in the comparator group, with the vehicle control showing no measurable improvement. Notably, no participants reported irritation, dryness, or discomfort, underscoring the serum's tolerability.

The significance of Hydrobiome Serum lies in its innovative formulation, which combines TCP with **sodium tocopheryl phosphate (TPNa)**. This combination is designed to modulate **membrane lipid signaling**, enhancing barrier integrity and hydration while reducing visible redness, all without broadly suppressing immune activity. This mechanism suggests a targeted approach to skin health that could have implications for aging skin and conditions characterized by compromised barrier function.

The introduction of Hydrobiome Serum could shift current paradigms in skincare research and product development, particularly in the context of **professional-grade treatments**. By focusing on the modulation of lipid signaling rather than traditional anti-inflammatory strategies, this product may pave the way for new therapeutic approaches in dermatology and longevity science. The exclusive distribution through licensed professionals also emphasizes a trend towards personalized skincare solutions, potentially impacting how future products are developed and marketed within the industry.
