King Faisal Specialist Hospital & Research Centre (KFSH) is set to participate as a Silver Sponsor at the 2026 Milken Institute Global Conference in Beverly Hills, California. The event will convene global leaders across health, finance, technology, policy, and academia to address pressing challenges, with a particular focus on advancing innovation and fostering intersectoral collaboration. Dr. Björn Zoëga, KFSH's Executive Deputy Director, will lead a specialized session on the future of cancer care, emphasizing the need for integrated, scalable, and outcome-driven oncology models.

The significance of KFSH's involvement lies in its commitment to transforming cancer care systems. Dr. Zoëga's session will advocate for a shift from isolated advancements to comprehensive system-level changes that prioritize **early detection**, **rapid intervention**, and **continuous learning** throughout the cancer care cycle. This approach aligns with the growing recognition that effective cancer management requires not only innovative treatments but also a robust infrastructure that supports patient outcomes and healthcare efficiency.

This engagement at the Milken Institute Conference underscores a critical paradigm shift in healthspan and longevity research, where the integration of diverse stakeholders is essential for translating scientific advancements into clinical practice. By participating in high-level discussions aimed at aligning capital, technology, and care delivery, KFSH is poised to influence the trajectory of healthcare innovation and accelerate the adoption of next-generation care models globally. The implications for drug development timelines and research focus are profound, as collaborative frameworks may lead to faster implementation of breakthrough therapies and improved patient outcomes in oncology.
