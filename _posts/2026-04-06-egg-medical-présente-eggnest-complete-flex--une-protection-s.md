Egg Medical has launched **EggNest™ Complete Flex**, a groundbreaking radiation protection system that allows clinicians to operate safely without traditional lead aprons or with ultra-lightweight alternatives. This innovative technology eliminates the need for structural modifications or interruptions to procedures, marking a significant advancement in occupational safety for healthcare professionals. The first live case utilizing Complete Flex will be performed by Dr. Jasvindar Singh at Barnes-Jewish Hospital on April 8, 2026, during the ARCH 2026 conference.

The significance of EggNest Complete Flex lies in its capacity to enhance clinician safety while maintaining operational efficiency. Unlike conventional ceiling-mounted radiation protection systems that require extensive renovations and can disrupt clinical workflows, Complete Flex offers a **no-installation** solution that preserves revenue by keeping procedure rooms operational. This system not only reduces costs associated with traditional protective measures but also addresses the growing need for improved clinician retention and reduced occupational risks in healthcare settings. Dr. George Chrysant emphasizes that enhanced radiation protection devices (ERPDs) should be standard practice for ensuring the safety of healthcare teams.

The introduction of EggNest Complete Flex is poised to shift current paradigms in radiation safety protocols, potentially accelerating the adoption of ERPDs across healthcare facilities. As hospitals face increasing pressure to balance clinician safety with operational demands, this technology provides a viable solution that does not compromise patient care or financial stability. With installations already in fifteen laboratories and plans for eighty more by the end of Q2, the momentum for integrating such systems into standard practice is gaining traction, setting a new benchmark for radiation protection in clinical environments.
