Recent discussions around NAD+ (Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) have gained traction as researchers explore its potential role in aging and healthspan enhancement. This coenzyme, critical for cellular metabolism, has been linked to various age-related conditions and is being investigated for its therapeutic applications in promoting longevity.

NAD+ levels naturally decline with age, which has spurred interest in supplementation and its implications for healthspan. Studies suggest that boosting NAD+ may improve mitochondrial function, enhance DNA repair mechanisms, and even influence the activity of sirtuins—proteins associated with longevity. This growing body of research highlights the potential for NAD+ precursors in clinical settings, particularly for age-related diseases and metabolic disorders.

For longevity professionals, understanding the mechanisms behind NAD+ and its impact on aging is crucial.
