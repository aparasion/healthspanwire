ClinNEXUS, a data-driven healthcare company, is set to play a pivotal role at the upcoming **Suncoast Ventures 2nd Annual Gateway Conference** on April 14-15 in San Francisco. The company, led by CEO **Greg Carroll, M.D.**, aims to redefine care coordination by integrating clinical, social, and behavioral support systems to enhance health outcomes. As a top sponsor, ClinNEXUS emphasizes the importance of collaboration among payers, providers, and policymakers to tackle systemic healthcare challenges that contribute to high costs and poor patient outcomes.

The conference will focus on actionable solutions that align with existing healthcare workflows and incentives. Dr. Carroll will participate in a panel titled **“It Takes the Whole Bench: Partnering for Community-Centered Care at Scale,”** where he will discuss the critical role of public-private partnerships and integrated workflows in reducing avoidable hospitalizations, particularly for vulnerable populations. This aligns with the goals of **Enhanced Care Management** as outlined by California's **CalAIM** initiative, which seeks to improve care access and quality since its launch in 2022.

The key takeaway from ClinNEXUS's involvement is the potential shift in research and implementation paradigms towards **community-centered care**. By leveraging generative AI insights and culturally competent care coordinators, ClinNEXUS is positioned to address social determinants of health—such as housing and food insecurity—that significantly impact health outcomes. This approach not only enhances the therapeutic landscape for underserved communities but also sets a precedent for future healthcare models that prioritize integrated, holistic care strategies in longevity and healthspan research.
