The Buck Institute for Research on Aging has launched Healthspan Horizons, an innovative initiative aimed at addressing the critical challenge of measuring and extending healthspan—the duration of life spent in good health. This initiative seeks to bridge the gap between the increasing lifespan of individuals and the stagnation of healthspan, which often leads to prolonged periods of chronic disease management. By leveraging artificial intelligence and real-world data, Healthspan Horizons aims to create a comprehensive research infrastructure that can identify the factors contributing to healthy aging.

Healthspan Horizons will utilize a federated model to integrate multi-modal data from various sources, including wearables, nutrition, and laboratory results. This approach allows for the collection of dense longitudinal datasets that can reveal subtle patterns in health and resilience over time. The initiative emphasizes ethical governance and privacy, enabling participants to contribute their data while maintaining control over its use. With insights derived from this rich data, researchers and healthcare providers can develop strategies for early intervention and disease prevention, ultimately supporting individuals in maintaining energy, strength, and independence as they age.

A key takeaway from this initiative is the potential for Healthspan Horizons to transform healthspan into a measurable and actionable metric within the longevity landscape. By fostering collaboration among researchers, clinicians, and individuals, the platform aims to democratize access to healthspan science, paving the way for a more informed and proactive approach to aging. As the initiative progresses, it holds the promise of redefining how we understand and enhance the quality of life in our later years, making healthspan a central focus of longevity research and practice.
