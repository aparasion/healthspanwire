NipsApp Game Studios, traditionally known for its mobile and PC game development, is pivoting towards **immersive technologies** with significant real-world applications. Founder and CEO Nipin P N has announced a strategic shift focusing on **AI-driven VR and AR** systems designed for training in high-stakes environments, including healthcare and industrial safety. This transition aims to leverage the company's expertise in engaging users to enhance training efficacy, particularly for medical professionals and workers in high-risk industries.

The company's initial focus is on **healthcare**, where they are developing VR-based training simulations that allow doctors to practice on **AI-generated cases** that mimic real patient behavior. This dynamic system adapts to the trainee's actions, providing real-time feedback and guidance, ultimately aiming to improve surgical training efficiency. The concept of “cold practice” is central to their approach, ensuring that professionals can experience a variety of scenarios in a safe environment before facing real-life challenges.

This shift in focus has broader implications for the **training landscape** across various sectors. By integrating advanced simulation technology into training protocols, NipsApp is poised to redefine how professionals prepare for critical situations. Their approach could significantly shorten training timelines and enhance preparedness, particularly in industries where errors can have catastrophic consequences. As NipsApp rolls out its first healthcare pilot later this year and plans for industrial partnerships in 2027, the success of these initiatives will hinge on their ability to establish trust in regulated fields, moving beyond traditional gaming metrics to deliver tangible, life-saving training solutions.
