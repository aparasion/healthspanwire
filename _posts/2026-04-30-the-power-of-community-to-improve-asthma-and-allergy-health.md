Researchers found that community support plays a crucial role in managing asthma and allergies, which affect over 106 million people in the U.S. During May, designated as National Asthma and Allergy Awareness Month, the Asthma and Allergy Foundation of America (AAFA) emphasizes the importance of collective efforts in improving health outcomes for those with these conditions. Many individuals with asthma and allergies struggle with daily symptoms that can lead to missed work and school, increased healthcare visits, and even fatalities. By fostering a supportive community, people can better manage their symptoms and improve their quality of life.

This year’s focus highlights how access to healthcare, local policies, and community resources can significantly influence asthma and allergy management. For example, living in smoke-free housing, having access to allergen-free food, and advocating for cleaner air can all help reduce triggers and improve overall health. The AAFA aims to empower individuals and families by providing resources, such as educational materials and community programs, to help them navigate their conditions more effectively.

The initiative is part of a broader advocacy effort, with events planned throughout May, including a press briefing at the U.S. Capitol on World Asthma Day. The AAFA will push for better access to affordable treatments, increased research funding, and stronger health protections, especially for children. While the evidence supporting community-based approaches is strong, ongoing advocacy and community engagement are essential to ensure lasting improvements in asthma and allergy care.

For those affected by asthma and allergies, engaging with local support networks and advocating for better health policies can make a significant difference in managing their conditions.
