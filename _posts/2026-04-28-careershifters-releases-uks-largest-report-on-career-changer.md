Researchers found that most people considering a career change are not motivated by ambition or higher salaries, but rather by a desire to escape work that harms their health and wellbeing. The newly released State of Career Change Report 2026, based on a survey of over 11,500 professionals in the UK, reveals that a staggering 73% of career changers feel their job negatively impacts their overall life satisfaction. Among these individuals, 91% report that their work affects their mental health. The primary reasons for seeking change include unsupportive work environments, poor work-life balance, and misalignment with personal values.

This report highlights the importance of workplace wellbeing for those looking to age well and maintain a high quality of life. People are increasingly recognizing that their jobs can be detrimental to their mental and physical health. With nearly 60% of respondents wanting to leave their current industry and many feeling stuck for years, this trend underscores the need for supportive work environments that prioritize mental health and personal fulfillment.

While the findings are based on a large sample of active career changers, they reflect self-selected individuals who may not represent the entire workforce. The report indicates that clarity is a significant barrier to change, with 49% of respondents unsure about what to pursue next. The call to action for individuals is clear: start exploring new opportunities, even if you don’t feel fully ready. Seeking structured support rather than relying solely on friends and family can lead to better outcomes.
