RTA Outdoor Living has launched **Health Haven**, a YouTube special that investigates the impact of residential outdoor environments on **stress reduction**, **recovery**, **physical activity**, and **social behavior**. Led by performance coach Justin Roethlingshoefer, the project showcases a real-world implementation of environmental health principles by transforming a one-acre backyard into a multifunctional wellness space. This initiative aligns with a growing body of research indicating that well-designed outdoor areas can facilitate healthier lifestyle choices and improve overall well-being.

The significance of this project is underscored by a **meta-analysis** of 143 studies published in *Environmental Research*, which found that exposure to green spaces is correlated with **lower cortisol levels** and enhanced **heart rate variability (HRV)**—key indicators of stress resilience and recovery. Research from the University of Exeter further supports these findings, linking access to private outdoor spaces with increased reported well-being and regular physical activity. The Health Haven project integrates features such as an outdoor kitchen, recovery areas, and spaces for movement and social gatherings, effectively demonstrating how environmental design can promote consistent health behaviors.

The implications for the field are substantial; this project exemplifies how **environmental design** can be harnessed to shift current paradigms in health promotion and wellness. By emphasizing the importance of accessibility and ease of use in outdoor spaces, it highlights a critical avenue for future research and development in healthspan interventions. As environments that support healthy behaviors become more integrated into daily life, the potential for sustained behavioral change and improved health outcomes increases, paving the way for innovative approaches in both residential design and public health initiatives.
