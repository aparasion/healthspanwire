Leto Tech PBC has launched an innovative software platform designed to enhance the efficiency of connecting clients with community resources, functioning as a customizable electronic version of a social worker's binder. Developed from research at Purdue University, this platform allows users to create tailored digital binders that can be searched by various criteria, including screening questions and keywords. The software aims to streamline the referral process to local social services, facilitating better care coordination among health centers and social service organizations.

The significance of this development lies in its potential to address the social determinants of health, which account for approximately **80%** of health outcomes. By improving access to community resources—such as food, transportation, and education—the platform can have a profound impact on health management, particularly for chronic conditions like diabetes. Leto's system was validated through extensive feedback from healthcare organizations in Indiana and was recognized for its effectiveness in a competitive setting, placing second among **134** participants in a federal HRSA competition focused on improving health care coordination.

The takeaway from Leto Tech's initiative is its potential to shift the paradigm of resource management in health care. By integrating this software into electronic health records and educational systems, organizations can enhance client engagement and outcomes through better resource allocation and relationship management. This approach not only addresses the immediate needs of clients but also provides a framework for analyzing the effectiveness of community resources, ultimately contributing to improved health metrics and educational success.
