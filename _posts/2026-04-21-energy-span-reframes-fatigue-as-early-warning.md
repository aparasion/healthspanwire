Dr. Noa Rappaport’s keynote at the Buck Institute’s Healthspan Horizons introduces **Energy Span** as a quantifiable signal of healthspan decline, challenging the traditional dismissal of fatigue as a mere consequence of aging. This concept reframes energy not as a subjective experience but as a **biological precursor** that reflects shifts in mitochondrial function, metabolic flexibility, and autonomic regulation long before clinical markers indicate deterioration. Rappaport emphasizes that while energy declines may precede overt disease, they remain among the least measured aspects of health, presenting an opportunity for early intervention.

The significance of this perspective lies in its potential to bridge the gap between patient experiences and clinical assessments. By recognizing **Energy Span** as an early warning system, researchers and clinicians can focus on **coordinated shifts** in biological systems that manifest as fatigue, rather than waiting for more severe symptoms to arise. This approach aligns with a broader shift in geroscience toward understanding aging as a dynamic process influenced by both cumulative wear and acute stressors. The dual model of gradual “drift” and abrupt “cliff events” highlights critical inflection points where intervention may be most effective, underscoring the importance of timely detection.

The takeaway from Rappaport’s insights is the potential for **Energy Span** to reshape research paradigms and clinical practices. By integrating advanced monitoring technologies and machine learning, this framework could transform how we track energy as a continuous metric rather than an episodic one. This shift not only emphasizes the need for personalized health strategies but also calls for systemic changes in how we structure work and recovery environments, recognizing that chronic fatigue is a public health issue. If validated, **Energy Span** could become a pivotal metric in both aging research and clinical practice, fostering a more nuanced understanding of vitality and healthspan.
