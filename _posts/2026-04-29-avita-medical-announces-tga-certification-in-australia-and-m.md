Researchers have developed a new system called RECELL GO that automates the preparation of RECELL Spray-On Skin, a treatment for burn and trauma wounds. This innovative technology has recently received regulatory approval in Australia and New Zealand, paving the way for its commercial launch. The system aims to streamline the process of preparing skin cells from a patient’s own healthy skin, which can then be sprayed onto wounds to promote healing.

For people seeking effective treatments for burns or trauma, RECELL GO could mean faster recovery times and less need for extensive skin grafts. By using a small sample of the patient’s skin, this method not only reduces pain and scarring but also shortens hospital stays. The system is designed to enhance the efficiency of the preparation process, which could significantly improve outcomes for patients in need of urgent care.

Currently, RECELL GO has been certified for use in both Australia and New Zealand, but it is still in the early stages of commercial rollout. While the technology is based on established methods and has received positive feedback from experts, it is important to note that widespread clinical evidence is still developing. As such, while this advancement is promising, it may take time before it becomes a standard treatment option in all healthcare settings.

If you or someone you know may benefit from advanced wound care, keep an eye on RECELL GO's availability in your region. Always consult with a healthcare professional to explore the best treatment options for your specific needs.
