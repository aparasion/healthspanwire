The **2026 Vail Hip Summit**, organized by the Steadman Philippon Research Institute (SPRI) in collaboration with the Hospital for Special Surgery (HSS), will take place from April 9-11 at the Four Seasons Resort Vail. This year marks the 19th iteration of the summit, which has evolved into a premier forum for hip preservation specialists. With over 25 distinguished faculty members, including Dr. Marc J. Philippon, the event will focus on the latest advancements in **hip repair, preservation, and rehabilitation**, featuring live surgical demonstrations and discussions on emerging research.

The summit serves as a critical platform for **multidisciplinary collaboration** and the exchange of innovative surgical techniques. Dr. Philippon emphasized the event's commitment to evolving content, stating that the discussions continually push the field forward. Dr. Ernest L. Sink will open the summit with sessions on pathomechanics and diagnosis, while Dr. Srino Bharam will lead discussions on patient outcomes, underscoring the importance of refining recovery pathways. This emphasis on clinical insights and diverse approaches fosters a rich environment for debate and learning among leading experts in hip surgery.

The implications of this summit extend beyond immediate clinical applications; it represents a shift toward a more integrated approach in **orthopaedics and sports medicine**. By gathering top minds in the field, the Vail Hip Summit not only highlights current best practices but also sets the stage for future research directions and potential therapeutic innovations. As hip preservation techniques continue to advance, the collaborative insights gained from this event could significantly impact patient care and recovery protocols in the coming years.
