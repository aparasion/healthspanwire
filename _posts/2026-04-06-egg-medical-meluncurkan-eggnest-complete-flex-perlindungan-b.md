Egg Medical has unveiled the **EggNest™ Complete Flex**, a groundbreaking advancement in radiation protection that allows medical professionals to operate safely without traditional lead aprons or extensive construction modifications. This innovative system will make its clinical debut in a live procedure conducted by Dr. Jasvindar Singh at Barnes-Jewish Hospital on April 8, 2026, during the ARCH 2026 meeting.

The significance of the EggNest Complete Flex lies in its ability to enhance **workplace safety** while minimizing operational disruptions. Unlike conventional radiation shielding systems that require substantial structural changes and downtime, Complete Flex can be installed without construction, allowing laboratories to maintain operations and safeguard potential revenue. This system not only reduces the physical strain on medical staff by eliminating the need for cumbersome protective gear but also addresses critical staffing retention and operational efficiency challenges within healthcare settings.

The introduction of this technology could reshape the landscape of radiation safety protocols in interventional cardiology and beyond. By providing a cost-effective solution that enhances safety without compromising workflow, the EggNest Complete Flex may accelerate the adoption of **Enhanced Radiation Protection Devices (ERPDs)** across healthcare facilities. As hospitals increasingly prioritize staff well-being and operational continuity, this innovation positions itself as a vital tool in advancing clinical practices while addressing the pressing need for improved working conditions in high-risk environments.
