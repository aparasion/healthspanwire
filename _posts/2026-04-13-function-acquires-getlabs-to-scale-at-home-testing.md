Function's acquisition of **Getlabs** marks a pivotal shift in healthcare delivery, enhancing the accessibility of health data by transforming diagnostics into an everyday experience. By integrating Getlabs' mobile healthcare services, Function enables licensed professionals to conduct lab tests directly at patients' homes or workplaces, effectively eliminating the traditional barriers of commuting and waiting rooms. CEO Jonathan Swerdlin emphasizes that this change is not merely about convenience but fundamentally about **access** to health information, which has historically been hindered by logistical challenges.

The significance of this acquisition lies in its potential to reshape the diagnostic landscape. By removing friction from the testing process, Function aims to facilitate timely insights into health conditions, which are critical for proactive health management. The seamless integration of Getlabs' services will allow members to choose between visiting lab locations or scheduling home visits, thereby normalizing regular health monitoring. This shift positions diagnostics as a **forefront activity**, transforming it from a reactive measure into a proactive component of health management, ultimately fostering a culture of continuous health awareness.

The broader implication of this development is a redefinition of healthcare engagement. As Function lowers the barriers to participation in health monitoring, it encourages individuals to integrate regular testing into their routines, making health data collection a habitual practice rather than an occasional task. This paradigm shift not only enhances individual health management but also aligns with the overarching goal of longevity research: to empower individuals with consistent data that informs their health decisions. As diagnostics move closer to patients, the potential for a more personalized and continuous healthcare experience emerges, fundamentally altering how we perceive and interact with health systems.
