Playground Pediatrics has announced its expansion across Georgia, North Carolina, Pennsylvania, and Tennessee, adding several independent pediatric practices to its network. This growth brings the total to 19 locations and over 70 healthcare providers, allowing them to care for more than 80,000 children each year. The aim is to improve access to quality pediatric care, especially in rural and developing areas, ensuring that families receive the support they need for their children's health.

For families seeking better healthcare options for their children, this expansion means easier access to comprehensive pediatric services. Practices like Purcell Pediatrics in North Carolina and Cobb Pediatrics in Georgia are now part of Playground Pediatrics, which enhances their ability to provide preventive care, immunizations, and urgent services. This is particularly important for parents who want to ensure their children receive timely medical attention and ongoing support for their health and well-being.

The evidence for the effectiveness of this model is still emerging. Playground Pediatrics is focused on supporting independent practices, allowing them to concentrate on patient care rather than the administrative burdens of running a business. While the initiative shows promise, it is still in the early stages of implementation, and its long-term impact on pediatric health outcomes remains to be fully assessed.

As this network grows, families can look out for new options in their communities. If you have children, consider exploring these new practices to find a provider that meets your family's needs and offers convenient access to care.
