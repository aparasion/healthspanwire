(OTCQB: DOGP) is strategically leveraging its majority ownership of PrestoDoctor to enhance its telehealth platform in light of recent federal reclassifications of marijuana-related products under U.S. law. With certain cannabis products now categorized under Schedule III of the Controlled Substances Act, PrestoDoctor is positioned to facilitate patient access to medical cannabis through remote consultations and certifications, aligning with state regulations. This operational framework not only enhances patient engagement but also establishes a recurring service model that could drive scalable growth.

The significance of DOGP's strategy lies in its ability to capitalize on the evolving regulatory landscape surrounding telehealth and medical cannabis. PrestoDoctor’s established infrastructure allows for compliant digital workflows, which are essential for navigating the complexities of state-regulated medical cannabis systems. As the federal government continues to review and potentially alter regulations, DOGP’s telehealth platform may play a crucial role in shaping patient access and healthcare delivery models in this emerging market.

The key takeaway for professionals in longevity science and healthspan research is the potential shift in telehealth paradigms that DOGP's approach represents. By expanding PrestoDoctor’s capabilities beyond cannabis consultations to include additional telehealth services, laboratory testing, and enhanced patient engagement models, DOGP is positioning itself to not only meet current regulatory requirements but also adapt to future healthcare delivery innovations. This adaptability could accelerate the integration of telehealth solutions within the broader healthcare ecosystem, ultimately benefiting patient care and outcomes in the longevity sector.
