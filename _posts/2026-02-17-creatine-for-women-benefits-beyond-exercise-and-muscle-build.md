Recent research highlights the multifaceted benefits of creatine supplementation for women, extending beyond its well-known role in exercise and muscle building. While traditionally associated with athletic performance, creatine is now being recognized for its potential neuroprotective properties, cognitive enhancement, and overall health benefits, particularly in women.

This shift in understanding aligns with growing trends in longevity science, where the focus is increasingly on optimizing healthspan through dietary supplements. Creatine's ability to support mitochondrial function and reduce oxidative stress may have implications for age-related decline and cognitive health, making it a promising candidate for further investigation in both clinical and research settings.

For longevity professionals, the emerging evidence for creatine's diverse benefits suggests a need for deeper exploration into its role in women's health, particularly as it relates to aging and neurodegenerative diseases. This could pave the way for novel therapeutic strategies aimed at enhancing quality of life across the lifespan.
