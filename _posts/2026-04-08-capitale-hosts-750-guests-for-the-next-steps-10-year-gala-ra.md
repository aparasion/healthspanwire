The **Next Step** organization recently celebrated a decade of impact at Capitale in New York City, highlighting its mission to support amputees through community and rehabilitation. The gala honored key contributors, including Stephen and Vered Shamosh and Dr. Avishai and Elisheva Neuman, while emphasizing the resilience of the amputee community. The event was not only a testament to the collective strength of the network but also a precursor to the launch of the **Next Step Empowerment Center**, a 6,500-square-foot facility set to open in Spring 2026.

The Empowerment Center aims to serve as a national hub for amputees in Israel, integrating **rehabilitation**, **mental health support**, and **fitness programs** under one roof. Key features will include advanced physical therapy facilities and adaptive fitness training spaces, designed to foster independence and community connection. This initiative reflects a paradigm shift in how recovery is approached for amputees, focusing on both **mental and physical empowerment**. Tzachi Liebovich, director of The Next Step Israel, emphasized that the center will redefine recovery, guiding individuals from initial rehabilitation to full reintegration into daily life through flagship programs like **Walk Again**, **Run Again**, and **Live Again**.

The implications of this development are significant for the field of rehabilitation and healthspan research. By creating a comprehensive support system that addresses both the physical and emotional needs of amputees, The Next Step is setting a new standard for rehabilitation models. This approach could influence future research and funding priorities, potentially accelerating the development of integrated care models that prioritize holistic recovery and community engagement in various therapeutic contexts.
