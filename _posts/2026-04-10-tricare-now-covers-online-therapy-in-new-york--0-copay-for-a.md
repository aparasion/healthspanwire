Manhattan Mental Health Counseling (MMHC) has expanded its telehealth services to accept **TRICARE Select** and **TRICARE Reserve Select**, providing military families in New York with access to licensed psychotherapy at **$0 copays** for active-duty dependents. This initiative aims to address the unique mental health challenges faced by military personnel and their families, including **PTSD**, **military sexual trauma (MST)**, and the stress of frequent relocations. With an average scheduling time of under **three days**, MMHC ensures timely access to care without the need for referrals, thereby reducing barriers to treatment.

The significance of this development lies in its potential to enhance mental health outcomes for a population that often encounters stigma and logistical hurdles in seeking care. MMHC's clinical team is specifically trained in military mental health, utilizing evidence-based therapies such as **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)** and **Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR)**. This tailored approach aligns with **VA/DoD Clinical Practice Guidelines**, ensuring that service members receive appropriate and effective treatment for their conditions. By managing the complexities of TRICARE eligibility and claims directly, MMHC alleviates the administrative burden on patients, allowing them to focus on their recovery.

This expansion not only shifts the landscape of mental health care for military families but also sets a precedent for other telehealth providers in the field. By demonstrating that specialized care can be delivered effectively through telehealth channels, MMHC paves the way for future research and development of similar models. The integration of military-experienced therapists into civilian telehealth services could catalyze broader acceptance of virtual care solutions, ultimately improving access to mental health resources for underserved populations.
