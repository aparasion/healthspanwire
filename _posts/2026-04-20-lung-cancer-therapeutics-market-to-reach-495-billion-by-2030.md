The **lung cancer therapeutics market** is set to expand significantly, projected to grow from **$30.2 billion in 2024 to $49.5 billion by 2030**, reflecting a **compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 8.8%** during the period from 2025 to 2030. This growth is primarily driven by the increasing incidence of lung cancer, largely attributed to lifestyle factors and an aging global population, coupled with unprecedented investments in research and development (R&D) that are yielding transformative therapeutic innovations.

The report highlights several key drivers of this market expansion. Notably, **North America leads the market**, accounting for **53.7%** of global sales, bolstered by its advanced healthcare infrastructure and a robust pharmaceutical industry presence. The **60% increase in R&D spending** by pharmaceutical companies between 2012 and 2022 has catalyzed the development of novel therapies, such as **antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs)** and **biospecific antibodies** targeting solid tumors. Additionally, government funding initiatives are enhancing treatment accessibility and supporting clinical trial infrastructure, further propelling market growth.

For professionals in longevity science and healthspan research, the implications of this market trajectory are significant. The convergence of **AI-driven drug discovery** and **precision medicine** is redefining treatment paradigms, suggesting a shift toward more personalized therapeutic approaches. This evolving landscape presents new opportunities for research and collaboration in developing targeted therapies, while also highlighting the need to navigate challenges posed by generic competition and regulatory changes. As the market matures, stakeholders should focus on leveraging innovative technologies to optimize patient outcomes and address the growing demand for effective lung cancer treatments.
