The Neuroscience of Vitality and Aging (NOVA) Conference, set for April 25, 2026, in Boston, aims to unite leaders from neuroscience, biotechnology, policy, and investment to tackle the pressing challenge of maintaining brain health throughout life and combating neurodegenerative diseases. Organized by the Aging Initiative, this one-day interdisciplinary event seeks to bridge the gap between foundational brain-aging science and its clinical applications, fostering collaboration that has often been fragmented across various sectors.

The significance of NOVA lies in its focus on neurodegenerative diseases, which are among the fastest-growing global health concerns. Despite advancements in neurotechnology and regenerative medicine, therapeutic progress has lagged. By facilitating direct dialogue among researchers, clinicians, policymakers, and industry professionals, NOVA emphasizes brain aging as a modifiable biological process rather than a mere consequence of aging. The conference will cover critical themes, including the development of innovative tools in neuroengineering and brain-computer interfaces, the impact of investment and policy on therapeutic innovation, and emerging clinical strategies for patient care.

One key takeaway from NOVA is its potential to catalyze interdisciplinary collaboration, which is essential for translating scientific discoveries into effective interventions. By bringing together diverse stakeholders, the conference aims to create a holistic understanding of brain aging and its implications for healthspan, ultimately driving forward the agenda for research and therapeutic development in this vital area.
