Researchers found that effective leadership in healthcare can significantly improve employee retention and engagement. A new report from Laudio, in partnership with the American Society for Healthcare Human Resources Administration, highlights how frontline managers play a crucial role in translating organizational strategies into everyday actions. As healthcare systems face increasing pressures, including workforce shortages and financial constraints, the quality of relationships between employees and their managers has become more important than ever.

For people working in healthcare, this means that strong connections with managers can lead to better job satisfaction and a greater desire to stay in their roles. The report indicates that regular, meaningful engagement is particularly vital for younger employees, such as those in Generation Z. With over 15,000 managers surveyed across more than 150 hospitals, the findings suggest that consistent, human-centered leadership is key to maintaining a stable workforce, rather than simply implementing new programs.

The evidence comes from a comprehensive analysis of healthcare management practices, emphasizing that while strategies are abundant, the challenge lies in effective execution at the frontline. The report outlines actionable steps for HR leaders, including reducing administrative burdens on managers and focusing on foundational leadership behaviors like communication and presence. By simplifying work processes and enhancing interpersonal connections, healthcare organizations can foster a more engaged workforce that ultimately benefits patient care.
