WELL Health has announced a strategic partnership with AliveCor to enhance cardiac care accessibility in Canada by integrating Canadian-registered cardiologists into AliveCor’s Kardia platform. This initiative allows users of the Kardia app to request paid clinician ECG reviews, which are conducted by certified cardiologists within 24 hours of ECG recording. The Kardia app leverages Health Canada-licensed AI algorithms to detect three prevalent arrhythmias in just 30 seconds, with the platform boasting over **350 million ECGs** recorded to date.

The significance of this collaboration lies in its potential to address the growing demand for cardiology services amidst increasing wait times for elective procedures, which have surged by **53%** to an average of **15.3 weeks**. By offering physician-validated interpretations and guidance, this partnership not only enhances the quality of remote cardiac monitoring but also creates a new revenue stream for WELL Health through clinician reviews. This model exemplifies a proactive approach to bridging gaps in healthcare access, particularly in cardiology, where specialist availability is often limited.

The takeaway from this partnership is its potential to reshape the landscape of cardiac care delivery in Canada. By integrating telehealth solutions with specialist input, it sets a precedent for future collaborations that leverage technology to improve patient outcomes. This model could accelerate the development of similar platforms in other areas of healthcare, ultimately influencing drug development timelines and clinical practices by fostering a more interconnected healthcare ecosystem.
