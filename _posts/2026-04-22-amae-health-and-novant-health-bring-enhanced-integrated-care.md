Amae Health is expanding its innovative outpatient care model for severe mental illness (SMI) to Charlotte, North Carolina, through a strategic partnership with Novant Health. This collaboration aims to enhance access to specialized care for patients with complex mental health conditions, such as schizophrenia and bipolar disorder, by opening a new clinic on May 18. The initiative builds on Amae's existing framework in Raleigh and emphasizes a **psychiatry-led, whole-person care model** that integrates mental health services with primary care, nutrition, and social support.

The significance of this expansion lies in its potential to improve patient outcomes through **continuity of care**. National research indicates that nearly **23% of SMI patients** are readmitted within 30 days of discharge. However, Amae's integrated approach has demonstrated a remarkable **30-day readmission rate below 4%**, underscoring the effectiveness of coordinated, multidisciplinary care. The collaboration with Novant Health allows for seamless transitions from inpatient to outpatient care, facilitated by a dedicated transition planner who will support patients during their hospitalization and ensure they receive comprehensive treatment post-discharge.

This partnership not only enhances the availability of mental health services but also shifts the paradigm of care delivery for SMI. By emphasizing **community-based treatment** and proactive engagement with patients, this model could influence future drug development timelines and research directions in mental health. The focus on integrated care may inspire similar collaborations across the country, ultimately leading to improved health outcomes and reduced healthcare costs for individuals living with severe mental illness.
