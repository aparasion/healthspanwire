Researchers at BioCardia, Inc. have developed a new imaging technology called Heart3D Fusion Imaging, which could enhance the treatment of heart failure. This innovative software combines preoperative 3D images of the heart from CT or MRI scans with real-time 2D images during procedures. This allows doctors to navigate and deliver cell therapies more effectively, potentially improving outcomes for people suffering from ischemic heart failure.

This advancement is particularly relevant for those looking to maintain heart health as they age. The CardiAMP Cell Therapy, which utilizes a patient’s own bone marrow cells, aims to improve heart function by increasing blood vessel density and reducing tissue scarring. If successful, this therapy could lead to better heart health, increased energy levels, and improved quality of life for individuals dealing with heart issues.

The research is still in the early stages, with Heart3D Fusion Imaging currently being used for investigational purposes only. While there have been positive indications from clinical trials, the technology has not yet been widely approved for use. The Japanese Pharmaceutical and Medical Device Agency has suggested that existing clinical data may support approval, but further validation is needed in larger populations.

For those interested in heart health, staying informed about new treatments like CardiAMP and Heart3D can be beneficial. While these technologies are not yet available for general use, understanding emerging therapies may help you discuss options with your healthcare provider in the future.
