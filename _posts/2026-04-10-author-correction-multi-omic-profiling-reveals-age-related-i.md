Correction has been issued for the article titled “Multi-omic profiling reveals age-related immune dynamics in healthy adults,” originally published in *Nature*. The correction addresses several labeling errors in Figures 2c and 2d, which have now been rectified in the updated HTML and PDF versions. This adjustment is crucial for accurately interpreting the data presented regarding immune system changes associated with aging.

The original study utilized multi-omic approaches to elucidate the complex immune dynamics that occur with age, highlighting significant shifts in immune cell populations and signaling pathways. These findings have implications for understanding the mechanisms underlying age-related diseases and could inform the development of targeted immunotherapies aimed at enhancing healthspan. The corrected figures provide clarity on the specific immune markers and their relationships, which are essential for researchers exploring therapeutic interventions in aging populations.

This correction underscores the importance of precision in data presentation, particularly in studies that inform future research directions and clinical applications. Accurate representation of data is vital for the credibility of findings and can influence subsequent drug development timelines and strategies in longevity research. Researchers should remain vigilant in ensuring data integrity to facilitate robust advancements in the understanding of aging and healthspan enhancement.
