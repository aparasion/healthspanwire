The global **infusion pumps and devices market** is projected to expand from **$9 billion in 2024 to $14.2 billion by 2030**, representing a **compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 7.9%**. This growth is driven by an increase in chronic diseases, higher surgical volumes, a rising elderly population, and a shift towards **ambulatory infusion pumps**. The report from BCC Research highlights the critical role these devices play in delivering medication for conditions such as diabetes, cancer, and cardiovascular diseases, which are becoming more prevalent.

The **significance** of these findings lies in the evolving healthcare landscape, where the demand for infusion therapy is intensifying. With **North America** holding a **42.5% market share**, the region benefits from advanced healthcare infrastructure and a strong home healthcare ecosystem. The integration of **smart infusion pumps** is reshaping the market, with innovations such as **dose error reduction systems (DERS)** and **wireless connectivity** enhancing patient safety and treatment efficacy. This technological advancement not only improves patient outcomes but also aligns with the broader trend of outpatient care, enabling patients to receive treatment in non-hospital settings.

The implications for research and development are substantial. The infusion pumps market is characterized by stable, recurring revenue driven by non-discretionary clinical needs, but it also faces challenges such as safety concerns and regulatory hurdles. Companies focusing on **smart technology**, interoperability with electronic health records (EHR), and expansion into home healthcare are well-positioned to capitalize on this growth. This shift emphasizes the need for ongoing innovation in infusion delivery systems, which could redefine treatment paradigms and accelerate the development of new therapeutic approaches in chronic disease management.
