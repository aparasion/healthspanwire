Claire’s Place Foundation is set to host the **12th Annual Glow Ride** on May 9, 2026, at Hermosa Beach Pier, celebrating the resilience of mothers within the cystic fibrosis (CF) community. This event, occurring during Mother’s Day weekend and Cystic Fibrosis Awareness Month, aims to unite families and supporters in a vibrant evening of illuminated rides, skating, and strolling along the beach, while honoring the strength of mothers like Claire Wineland, the foundation’s namesake, who lived with CF and advocated for others.

The Glow Ride serves not only as a community gathering but also as a critical fundraising initiative for the foundation’s programs, which include **financial assistance for families during hospital stays** and career support for adults living with CF through the Work Proudly Program. By spotlighting the lived experiences of families affected by CF, including former Hermosa Beach Mayor Stacey Armato, the event raises awareness and fosters a sense of solidarity among participants. The evening will feature various family-friendly activities, including music, face painting, and a special Mother’s Day photo backdrop, creating an inclusive atmosphere for all ages.

The implications of this event extend beyond community engagement; it highlights the importance of **support networks** for families dealing with chronic illnesses like CF. By bringing visibility to the challenges faced by these families, the Glow Ride reinforces the need for ongoing research and funding in the field of cystic fibrosis. As Claire’s Place Foundation marks its 15th anniversary, the Glow Ride exemplifies how grassroots initiatives can drive awareness and support for critical health issues, ultimately influencing the trajectory of care and research in the longevity and healthspan domains.
