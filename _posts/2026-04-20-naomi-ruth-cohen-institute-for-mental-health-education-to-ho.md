The **Naomi Ruth Cohen Institute for Mental Health Education** (NRCI) has announced its 24th Annual Community Mental Health Conference titled **"Alone Together: Confronting Loneliness Across the Lifespan,"** scheduled for May 16, 2026. This full-day event aims to address the growing public health crisis of loneliness, as highlighted in a 2023 advisory from the U.S. Surgeon General. The conference will feature a diverse array of speakers, including researchers and practitioners, who will explore the impact of loneliness on individuals from childhood through older adulthood, emphasizing the need for community connection and support.

The significance of this conference lies in its focus on **practical strategies** to combat loneliness and social isolation. With sessions covering various contexts—such as workplace dynamics, late-life issues, and cultural considerations—the conference seeks to equip attendees with evidence-based tools to foster belonging and well-being. Notably, the inclusion of a panel moderated by Dr. Alisha DeWalt will facilitate discussions on how life transitions and cultural expectations contribute to feelings of disconnection, providing a comprehensive understanding of loneliness across different demographics.

The key takeaway from this initiative is its potential to **shift current paradigms** in mental health research and practice. By emphasizing the multifaceted nature of loneliness and its implications for healthspan, the conference encourages professionals to integrate social connection strategies into clinical settings and community programs. This approach not only aligns with emerging research on the psychosocial determinants of health but also accelerates the timeline for developing interventions that address the mental health needs of diverse populations.
