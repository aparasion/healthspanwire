Recent insights from Banner Health highlight the potential benefits of creatine supplementation for women and adults over 40. This demographic, often experiencing age-related muscle loss and cognitive decline, may find creatine a valuable addition to their health regimen. The article discusses how creatine can enhance muscle mass, improve strength, and support cognitive function, making it particularly relevant for those focused on maintaining healthspan as they age.

The significance of these findings lies in the growing interest in non-pharmaceutical interventions for age-related decline. As research continues to explore the role of dietary supplements in promoting longevity, creatine emerges as a promising candidate. It provides a practical approach for individuals looking to counteract the effects of aging on muscle and brain health.

For longevity professionals, the takeaway is clear: creatine supplementation could serve as a simple yet effective strategy to enhance healthspan, warranting further investigation and consideration in clinical practice.
