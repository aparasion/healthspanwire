Researchers have developed a new 3D printing solution for dentures that could significantly improve the quality of life for people with missing teeth. The NextDent Jetted Denture Solution, which includes a specialized printer and materials, has received regulatory approval in Europe and will be available starting May 4, 2026. This technology allows dental labs to create customized, durable dentures in a single print, enhancing comfort and aesthetics for users.

This advancement is particularly relevant for the over 60 million people in the U.S. and Europe who are edentulous, meaning they are missing some or all of their teeth. By providing a more efficient and precise method for creating dentures, the NextDent solution could lead to better-fitting and more visually appealing dentures, ultimately improving users' confidence and overall well-being. Early feedback from U.S. users indicates high satisfaction with the aesthetics and comfort of these new dentures.

The certification of the NextDent Jetted Denture Solution marks a significant step in dental technology, but it's important to note that this is a commercial launch rather than a clinical trial. While the initial feedback is promising, further studies will be necessary to fully understand the long-term benefits and effectiveness of this new approach. As this technology rolls out in Europe, it could reshape how dentures are produced and fitted, making the process quicker and more accessible.

For those interested in dental health, keeping an eye on innovations like the NextDent solution could be beneficial, especially if you or someone you know is seeking alternatives to traditional dentures.
